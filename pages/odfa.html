ODFA - U01DE033978

Ontology of Dental care-related Fear, Anxiety, and/or Phobia


Principal Investigators

Bill Duncan and Dan McNeil

Overview

This research aims to create a controlled vocabulary for terms defining and classifying dental-care related fear, anxiety, and/or phobia (DFA). We do this by developing the Ontology of Dental care-related Fear, and Anxiety, and/or Phobia (ODFA). The ODFA's concepts and relations facilitate the development of tools and resources capable of enhancing our understanding of the impacts of DFA, and when applied to study data, the ODFA enables the integration and interoperability of data from multiple studies

Aims

1.

Develop and validate the Ontology of Dental care-related Fear, Anxiety, and/or Phobia (ODFA) using existing ontologies and data resources containing relevant concepts, relating the ODFA to other ontologies in the Behavioral and Social Science Ontology Development U01 Network

2.

Integrate and analyze multiple DFA research data sources using ODFA's concepts to harmonize differing semantics among the data sources' data fields and values

3.

Collect and analyze data using the newly developed ODFA to predict delayed treatment, as well as cancellations and no-shows at dental appointments
